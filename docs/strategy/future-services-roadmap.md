# OperateAI — Future Services Roadmap

**Date:** 2026-06-22
**Status:** Strategy draft (research-backed)
**Scope:** What OperateAI builds and sells *after* the first clients are onboarded on the free check → AI-visibility setup. Grounded in the company ethos (the relationship is the asset; be the bridge, not the builder; distribution before product; vertical depth before horizontal reach; sell present value honestly; access is the moat) and in current market research (sources cited inline; figures flagged where directional).

---

## 1. Where we are now (the base)

- **Audience:** WA tradies (one narrow community, deep).
- **Wedge (free):** the AI Visibility Check — name + suburb → a human-reviewed rundown of what AI says about them and the one thing costing them jobs. Now also auto-draftable server-side.
- **First paid offer:** done-for-you "AI-Findable Setup" (Google Business Profile made machine-perfect, plain-text AI-readable info, reviews engine, fast response).

Everything below is the expansion *on top of that relationship and that channel*. We never lead with the product; the audience and the trust come first, then we assemble what helps them.

---

## 2. The research basis (why now, and why this shape)

**The shift is real and fast.**
- Google AI Overviews went from ~6.5% of queries (Jan 2025) to ~48% (early 2026); Australia is *above* the global average since the Oct 2024 launch. [SQ Magazine; Search Engine Land; Omni Eclipse AU]
- Gartner forecasts a **~25% decline in traditional search volume by end of 2026**. [widely cited, AU sources]
- AU-specific: **49% of Australians used generative AI in the past year; 46% of Google searches have local intent.** [National AI Centre; AP Web Solutions]
- AI-referred visitors convert at **~4.4× organic** rates. [Semrush via Omni Eclipse — directional]

**The opportunity is wide open.**
- Only **~1.2% of local businesses are cited by AI**, and 95% that appear show up on only *one* of the four major engines — fragmented, winner-takes-most, and most tradies haven't heard of the problem. [Evolve AMZ; Demand Convert — directional but consistent]
- What AI reads is **third-party trust signals** (reviews, directories, schema/FAQ) — *not* the business's own website. Reviews ~3×, FAQ schema ~2.8×, content fresh <30 days ~3.2× more likely to be cited. [Evolve AMZ — directional]

**The future is agentic booking, and it's close.**
- Google AI Mode already completes bookings in-search; **Apple Siri ships multi-step local booking (find → compare → book) in autumn 2026**; OpenAI hands transactions to partners like Thumbtack. [DesignRush; Memeburn; Digital Commerce 360]
- So the arc is: **get recommended (now) → get booked by AI (12–24 months).** Our forward-position ("soon it books you") is becoming present tense on a known timeline.

**The build/buy/affiliate verdict (from the tooling scan).**
- Mature, funded incumbents already own voice AI, review automation, GBP tools, and job-management (Jobber, ServiceM8, Goodcall, NiceJob, BrightLocal). **Don't rebuild them.**
- Nearly all have **referral/affiliate/white-label** programs: ServiceM8 ($500 + up to 20% ongoing rev-share), Tradify ($160/referral), simPRO ($200), NiceJob ($150), BrightLocal (15% lifetime), GoHighLevel white-label ($497/mo, set-your-own-margin). [vendor partner pages]
- This is the ethos in market form: **earn from the incumbents' rails instead of fighting them.**

## 3. The expansion ladder (per-client, land-and-expand)

Each rung grows a client's lifetime value and is only offered once the previous rung has proven value. Prices in AUD, positioned deliberately below agency rates (solo operator, low overhead) — final pricing is the operator's call.

## 4. The bridge / affiliate layer (compounding, passive)

Per the ethos, the cleanest money is **earning from the incumbents your clients already use or need**. One client relationship can generate stacked, recurring referral income with no extra build:

- **Job management:** ServiceM8 ($500 + ~20% ongoing), Tradify ($160), simPRO ($200), Jobber (PartnerStack). Most tradies need one; you're the trusted advisor who recommends + implements.
- **Reviews:** NiceJob ($150/referral, reseller tier available).
- **GBP / local tools:** BrightLocal (15% lifetime, when reopened).
- **Orchestration:** GoHighLevel white-label ($497/mo) as your own branded automation layer (missed-call, chat, SMS) resold at margin.

**Positioning rule:** only ever recommend what genuinely helps the tradie (the BS-detector audience punishes anything else). The affiliate income is a by-product of honest assembly, never the reason for a recommendation.

## 5. Productized & data-moat plays (scalable beyond 1:1 services)

These leverage the `check-cli` pipeline and the proprietary data it accumulates.

- **"AI Visibility Score" (productized monitoring):** a low-cost monthly automated report (the check, run on a schedule) — a recurring, near-zero-marginal-cost product that also nurtures leads toward the managed tiers. Built on the existing pipeline.
- **The data moat (the real long-term asset):** every check logs *how a WA trade business looks to AI*. After 50–100 checks you own a dataset nobody else has — "X% of Perth sparkies have their services as one line," "the average plumber appears on 0.8 of 4 engines." That dataset becomes:
  - the **content/authority engine** (articles, the next hooks, PR — "we checked 200 Perth tradies, here's what we found"),
  - benchmarking inside client reports ("you're on 1 of 4 engines; the top tradies in your suburb are on 3"),
  - eventually a **self-serve "check + score" tier** for tradies who won't pay for done-for-you yet (top-of-funnel at scale).
- **Guardrail:** keep automation human-in-the-loop where the brand promise is "a real person checks it." Productize the *monitoring*, not the *trust*.

## 6. Horizontal expansion (only after WA tradies are won)

The ethos is explicit: **dominate one community before touching a second.** Sequence, do not parallelise:
1. **Win WA tradies** (the current focus) — be *the* name for "getting found by AI" among Perth trades.
2. **Adjacent WA local-service verticals** that share the exact same AI-discovery problem and tooling: health clinics, allied health, real estate, beauty/wellness, hospitality. Same playbook, new community, near-zero new product.
3. **Trades in other AU cities** (replicate the WA model) — only once the WA motion is systematised and partly automated.

Each new vertical/region gets its own check, its own data moat, its own first-deposit article. Reuse everything; rebuild nothing.

## 7. Sequencing & triggers (when to add each)

- **Now → first ~10 clients:** run Tiers 0–1 by hand. Prove the offer. Start logging the data moat. Begin one affiliate relationship (ServiceM8 or NiceJob) to monetise recommendations you're already making.
- **~10–20 clients / process proven:** launch Tier 2 (Managed Visibility retainer) for recurring revenue; semi-automate the check (the CLI/web auto-draft is already built); publish the first data-moat article.
- **Agentic booking signals strengthen (Apple Siri autumn 2026 / Google expansion):** launch Tiers 3–4 (Capture/Speed + Booking-Ready), mostly via white-label/affiliate. This is the forward-position turning present.
- **Retainer base stable + capacity strained:** launch Tier 5 premium managed + the productized "AI Visibility Score"; consider the first adjacent vertical.

## 8. Risks & guardrails

- **Platform dependency:** AI engines change ranking/citation logic and could absorb the layer (e.g. Google "just fixes" profiles). Mitigation — the *relationship and the trust* are the asset, not the technique; sell outcomes, stay tool-agnostic, keep the affiliate bridge so we win regardless of which tool wins.
- **AI non-determinism / over-promising:** never guarantee rankings or jobs; always frame results as a dated snapshot (already in the Terms). One oversell burns the BS-detector audience.
- **Over-building:** the temptation is to build voice AI / a CRM / a review tool. Don't — the research is unanimous that incumbents own these and the engineering cost is prohibitive. Assemble and affiliate.
- **Spreading thin:** resist horizontal expansion until WA tradies are genuinely won. Trying to help everyone is how you defend nothing.
- **Cost/abuse of automation:** the auto-draft check costs real money per run; keep it gated and monitored (already done).

## 9. What we deliberately do NOT build

- Voice AI / AI receptionist (refer Jobber/Goodcall/Smith.ai, or white-label GHL).
- Field-service / job-management software (affiliate ServiceM8/Tradify/simPRO).
- A review platform (affiliate NiceJob/Podium).
- A horizontal "AI for all SMBs" product (the trap the ethos was built to avoid).

---

## 10. The through-line

We are a trust-and-distribution business. Every free check deposits trust into the one account that compounds; every check logs data into the one moat we own; every incumbent we assemble pays us instead of competing with us. The services above are simply the increasingly valuable things we sell *to a community that already believes us* — in the order they're ready to buy them, at the moment the market makes each one urgent.
