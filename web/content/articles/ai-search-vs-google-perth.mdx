People keep asking us whether AI search "replaces" Google, and whether they need to throw out everything they've done on SEO. The honest answer is no: they overlap more than they differ. But the differences that exist are the ones that decide whether you get the customer. Here's the side-by-side.

## What's the core difference between AI search and Google search?

Google search hands the customer a list of options and lets them choose: ten blue links, a map pack, some ads. AI search skips the list. Ask ChatGPT or Google's AI answer for a good plumber and it names two or three and tells the customer why. Google helps people choose; AI chooses for them. That single shift changes everything downstream.

## AI search vs Google search, side by side

Here's how the two compare on the things that actually matter to a local business:

| What matters | Old Google search | AI search |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What the customer sees | A list of ~10 results plus ads and a map pack | A shortlist of two or three businesses, named |
| Who makes the choice | The customer, by scrolling and comparing | The AI, before the customer sees options |
| Is there a "page two"? | Yes, you can rank lower and still be found | No, you're named or you're invisible |
| Main thing it reads | Your website, keywords, backlinks | Your Google Business Profile, reviews, directories, plain-text info |
| How you win | Rank high enough to get clicked | Be clean and consistent enough to get named |
| Where the proof comes from | Mostly your own pages and links to them | Mostly third-party: reviews, directories, social, forums |
| What kills you | Being buried on page two | Being unreadable or inconsistent, so the AI skips you |

## Is AI search actually replacing Google for local businesses?

Not replacing, layering on top. Google still drives huge local volume, and Google itself now puts an AI-generated answer above the normal results on a large share of searches, so the two are merging. The practical takeaway: a customer increasingly gets a recommendation before they ever scroll. Whether that recommendation comes from ChatGPT or from Google's own AI box, the dynamic is the same: a shortlist, not a list.

## Does old-school SEO still matter, or is it dead?

It still matters, it's just no longer enough. Classic SEO got you onto the list and let the customer pick. That still happens for the people who scroll. But more and more of them don't scroll: they take the AI's shortlist at face value. So SEO that gets you to position six is worth a lot less than it used to be, because position six is below the AI answer that already named someone else. You need both: rank well and be the named pick.

## What do AI and Google reward in common?

Here's the good news, and the reason you don't have to pick a side. The fundamentals that win AI are the same ones that have always won local Google:

- **A complete, accurate Google Business Profile.** Google's own ranking signal and the single biggest thing AI leans on. Every service spelled out, exact service area, right categories, real hours.
- **Plain-text information.** Services, prices and areas in actual words, not locked inside a JPG or PDF. Google can't index an image and AI can't read one.
- **Recent, replied-to reviews.** A ranking factor on Google and one of the heaviest things AI weighs when choosing between two businesses.
- **Consistency everywhere.** Same name, address and details across your site, Maps and directories. Mismatched details hurt Google rankings and make AI hesitate to name you.

Do these and you're not choosing between Google and AI. You're winning both with one set of work.

> The work that gets you named by AI is the same work that's always won local Google. You're not picking a side, you're doing the fundamentals well.

## What's the one thing that's genuinely new with AI?

The off-site weight. In our own test of 48 Perth searches across three AI engines, just over half of everything the AI cited was directories and reviews (Facebook, hipages, Yelp, Reddit, Oneflare), not business websites. Google reads your site closely; AI reads what the rest of the web says about you. So a thin Facebook page or an empty hipages profile costs you more in the AI world than it ever did on Google. (We wrote up that test in full: [we asked three AI engines for the best tradies in Perth](/articles/we-asked-chatgpt-best-tradies-perth/).)

## So what should a Perth business do?

Don't abandon Google and don't panic about AI. Do the fundamentals that serve both: finish your Google Business Profile properly, put everything in plain text, build recent reviews, and make sure your details match everywhere AI looks. That's the whole playbook, and it future-proofs you whichever way search keeps tilting.
